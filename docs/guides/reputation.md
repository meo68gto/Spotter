# Reputation Guide

Understanding and building your Spotter reputation score.

## Overview

Your reputation score (0-100) reflects your standing in the Spotter community. Higher scores unlock benefits and increase trust with other members.

## Reputation Score Components

Your overall score is calculated from six weighted components:

| Component | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Ratings Average** | 30% | Average ratings from other players |
| **Network Size** | 20% | Number of quality connections |
| **Completion Rate** | 15% | Rounds completed vs. committed |
| **Referrals** | 15% | Successful referrals made |
| **Profile Completeness** | 10% | Percentage of profile filled |
| **Attendance Rate** | 10% | On-time arrival percentage |

## Component Breakdown

### Ratings Average (30%)

After each round, players rate each other:
- **5 stars** = Excellent (punctual, friendly, good sport)
- **4 stars** = Good
- **3 stars** = Average
- **2 stars** = Below average
- **1 star** = Poor (no-show, rude, slow play)

**How it's calculated:**
- Average of all ratings received
- Weighted by recency (recent ratings count more)
- Minimum 3 ratings for full weight

**Tips:**
- Be punctual to tee times
- Be friendly and welcoming
- Play ready golf
- Follow course etiquette
- Rate others fairly (they rate you back)

### Network Size (20%)

Quality connections boost your reputation:
- **0-10 connections:** Low score
- **11-50 connections:** Medium score
- **51-100 connections:** High score
- **100+ connections:** Maximum score

**Quality factors:**
- "Played Together" connections weighted higher
- Active connections (recent interaction)
- Mutual connections with other reputable members

**Tips:**
- Connect with people you actually play with
- Maintain your network (don't just collect)
- Make introductions between connections

### Completion Rate (15%)

Percentage of rounds you complete after committing:

```
Completion Rate = (Completed Rounds / Committed Rounds) × 100
```

**Scoring:**
- 95-100% = Excellent
- 80-94% = Good
- 60-79% = Fair
- Below 60% = Poor

**What counts against completion:**
- No-shows (didn't show up)
- Late cancellations (within 24 hours)
- Leaving early without valid reason

**Tips:**
- Only commit to rounds you can attend
- Cancel early if plans change
- Communicate with organizer if running late

### Referrals (15%)

Successful referrals to Spotter:

**What counts:**
- Invite someone who joins Spotter
- They complete profile
- They play at least one round

**Scoring:**
- Each successful referral: +15 points
- Maximum component score at 10 referrals

**Tips:**
- Invite golfers you know
- Help them complete profile
- Play a round together after they join

### Profile Completeness (10%)

Percentage of your profile completed:

**Basic Profile (FREE):**
- Display name: 5 points
- Bio: 5 points
- Avatar: 5 points
- Location: 5 points

**Professional Identity (SELECT+):**
- Company: 10 points
- Title: 10 points
- Industry: 10 points

**Golf Identity (SELECT+):**
- Handicap: 15 points
- Home course: 10 points
- Play frequency: 10 points
- Years playing: 10 points

**Tips:**
- Fill out all sections
- Update when information changes
- Complete profile = more connections

### Attendance Rate (10%)

Percentage of rounds you arrive on time:

**On time means:**
- Arrived 15+ minutes before tee time
- Checked in at course
- Ready to play at scheduled time

**Scoring:**
- 95-100% = Excellent
- 80-94% = Good
- Below 80% = Needs improvement

**Tips:**
- Plan to arrive 30 minutes early
- Account for traffic/parking
- Check in immediately upon arrival

## Common Reputation Issues

### Score Dropped Suddenly

**Possible causes:**
- No-show penalty (-20)
- Negative rating received (-15)
- Connection removed (-3)

**Recovery:**
- Complete next few rounds on time
- Get positive ratings
- Rebuild gradually

### Low Ratings Component

**If consistently rated poorly:**
- Arrive earlier
- Be more social/friendly
- Play faster (ready golf)
- Follow etiquette
- Ask for feedback

### Low Completion Rate

**If below 80%:**
- Be more selective committing
- Cancel earlier when needed
- Communicate proactively
- Set calendar reminders

## FAQ

**Q: How often does reputation update?**
A: Real-time for events, full recalculation every 24 hours.

**Q: Can reputation decrease?**
A: Yes, negative events reduce your score.

**Q: Is reputation visible to others?**
A: Yes, your tier (⭐-⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐) is public. Detailed breakdown is private.

**Q: Can I dispute a rating?**
A: No, ratings are subjective. Focus on future rounds.

**Q: Do organizer events affect reputation?**
A: Yes, hosting successful events boosts reputation.

**Q: What if I'm unfairly rated?**
A: One bad rating won't hurt much. Consistency matters more.

**Q: Does tier affect reputation?**
A: Tier upgrade gives +50 points. Otherwise separate systems.
