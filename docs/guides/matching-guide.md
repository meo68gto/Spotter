# Matching Guide: Understanding Compatibility

Complete guide to connection matching, compatibility factors, and building meaningful golf relationships on Spotter.

## Overview

Spotter's matching system goes beyond simple location-based discovery. It analyzes multiple compatibility factors to suggest connections that lead to enjoyable rounds and valuable relationships.

---

## How Matching Works

### The Matching Algorithm

Spotter evaluates compatibility across five dimensions:

1. **Golf Compatibility** (40%)
2. **Professional Alignment** (25%)
3. **Social Fit** (20%)
4. **Activity Patterns** (10%)
5. **Network Effects** (5%)

### Match Score Calculation

Each potential match receives a score (0-100):
- **90-100**: Excellent match
- **70-89**: Strong match
- **50-69**: Moderate match
- **Below 50**: Weak match

## Golf Compatibility (40%)

### Handicap Matching

**Why it matters:** Similar skill levels lead to more enjoyable rounds.

| Handicap Diff | Compatibility | Recommendation |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| 0-5 strokes | Excellent | Ideal playing partners |
| 6-10 strokes | Good | Competitive, some coaching possible |
| 11-15 strokes | Moderate | May use different tees |
| 16+ strokes | Challenging | Focus on social aspect |

**Tips:**
- Don't avoid better players (learning opportunity)
- Be honest about your handicap
- Consider using different tees if gap is large

### Play Frequency

Matching schedules is crucial:

| Your Frequency | Best Match | Notes |
|----------------|------------|-------|
| Daily | Daily/Weekly | Consistent availability |
| Weekly | Weekly/Biweekly | Regular weekend games |
| Biweekly | Weekly/Monthly | Flexible weekends |
| Monthly | Monthly/Occasionally | Quality over quantity |

### Playing Style

Beyond the numbers:
- **Competitive vs. Casual**: Match intensity
- **Fast vs. Leisurely**: Pace compatibility
- **Walking vs. Cart**: Physical preference
- **Drinking/Not**: Social compatibility

## Professional Alignment (25%)

### Industry Connections

Golf + business is a powerful combination:

**High-Value Industries for Golf Networking:**
- Finance (banking, investing)
- Real Estate (commercial, residential)
- Technology (SaaS, startups)
- Healthcare (medical devices, pharma)
- Legal (corporate, litigation)

### Professional Level

Matching career stages:
- **Entry level**: Learning, mentorship
- **Mid-career**: Peer networking, deals
- **Senior/Executive**: Strategic relationships
- **Entrepreneur**: Investment, partnerships

### Company Size

Sometimes matters, sometimes doesn't:
- Startups → Startups (shared struggles)
- Enterprise → Enterprise (similar scale)
- Mixed can work (complementary perspectives)

## Social Fit (20%)

### Communication Style

**What to look for:**
- Profile tone (serious vs. playful)
- Bio content (professional vs. personal)
- Photo style (formal vs. casual)

### Shared Interests

Beyond golf:
- Sports (other athletic interests)
- Travel (golf destinations)
- Food/Wine (19th hole compatibility)
- Family status (kids? flexibility?)

### Age Range

While not everything, similar life stages help:
- **20s-30s**: Building career, less flexible
- **40s-50s**: Peak earning, established
- **60+**: More time, different priorities

### Course Preferences

Where you play:

Regional patterns:
- **Snowbirds**: Winter golf travelers

- **Year-round**: Dedicated enthusiasts

## Compatibility Red Flags

### Signs of Poor Match

**Golf Mismatches:**
- Very different handicaps with no flexibility
- Opposite play frequencies
- Conflicting course preferences

**Professional Mismatches:**
- Competitors in same niche
- Unrelated industries with no overlap
- Conflicting business interests

**Social Mismatches:**
- Opposite personalities (too extreme)
- No common ground beyond golf
- Communication style clashes

## Introduction Requests (SELECT+)

### When to Request Introductions

**Good scenarios:**
- Strong profile match, no mutual connections
- Specific business reason
- Admire their game/reputation

**How to succeed:**
1. Choose the right connector (strong mutual)
2. Be specific about why
3. Offer value in return
4. Follow up promptly

### Responding to Introductions

**As the connector:**
- Only introduce if you know both well
- Provide context for both parties
- Stay out of it after introduction

**As the recipient:**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Thank the connector
- Be clear about your interest level

## Building Successful Connections

### The First Interaction

**Best practices:**
1. **Reference the match**: "I noticed we both..."
2. **Be specific**: Mention something from their profile
3. **Suggest next step**: Round, call, or event
4. **Set timeline**: "This weekend?" vs. "Sometime..."

### First Round Together

**Do:**
- Arrive early, be prepared
- Be positive regardless of play
- Find common ground
- Exchange contact info
- Plan next round

**Don't:**
- Give unsolicited swing advice
- Be overly competitive
- Complain about conditions
- Discuss business too aggressively

### Nurturing the Relationship

**Week 1:** Follow up message about the round
**Week 2-4:** Schedule next round
**Month 2+**: Regular playing partners

## Tier-Based Matching Strategies

### FREE Tier Strategy

**Limited resources, maximize impact:**
- Be selective: Quality over quantity
- Join rounds: Meet multiple people at once
- Focus locally: Minimize travel
- Complete profile: Stand out in search

**Connection limit (50):**
- Reserve for actual playing partners
- Remove inactive connections
- Prioritize active golfers

### SELECT Tier Strategy

**More tools, broader reach:**
- Use introduction requests strategically
- Filter by professional criteria
- Create rounds to attract similar players
- Build diverse network

**Connection limit (500):**
- Segment by relationship type
- Tag connections (golf buddy, business, both)
- Regular network pruning

### SUMMIT Tier Strategy

**Full access, leadership role:**
- Use priority placement
- Mentor newer members
- Host exclusive rounds
- Build influential network

**Unlimited connections:**
- Quality always matters
- Maintain active relationships
- Be the connector for others

## Matching FAQ

**Q: Why did someone decline my connection request?**
A: Could be various reasons: full network, different priorities, or simply not the right fit. Don't take it personally.

**Q: Should I connect with someone much better than me?**
A: Absolutely! Golfers love to share knowledge. Just be respectful of their time.

**Q: How many connections should I have?**
A: Focus on quality. Better to have 20 active playing partners than 200 dormant connections.

**Q: Can I remove a connection?**
A: Yes, go to their profile → Remove Connection. They won't be notified.

**Q: What if we play together and don't click?**
A: Not every match works. Be polite, finish the round, and don't feel obligated to connect.

**Q: How do I improve my match scores?**
A: Complete your profile, be active, and build your reputation.

## Tips for Different Matching Goals

### Building a Regular Group

**Look for:**
- Similar schedules (weekly availability)
- Compatible skill levels
- Local to same courses
- Social personalities

**Approach:**
- Join their rounds first
- Build rapport over multiple games
- Suggest becoming regulars

### Business Networking

**Look for:**
- Complementary industries
- Decision-maker roles
- Professional tone
- Active on platform

**Approach:**
- Lead with professional context
- Suggest business + golf
- Follow up after round

### Finding Playing Partners

**Look for:**
- Immediate availability
- Same course preferences
- Flexible handicaps
- Responsive to messages

**Approach:**
- Be direct about timing
- Join public rounds
- Build from there
