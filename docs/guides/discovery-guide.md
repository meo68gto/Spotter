# Discovery Guide: Finding Golf Partners

Complete guide to discovering and connecting with other golfers on Spotter.

## Overview

Spotter's discovery system helps you find golf partners who match your preferences, skill level, and networking goals. Same-tier visibility ensures you're always connecting with compatible members.

---

## Understanding Tier Visibility

### Same-Tier Rule

Spotter uses **same-tier visibility**:
- You can see and connect with members in your tier
- You can see members in higher tiers
- You cannot see members in lower tiers

### Visibility by Tier

| Your Tier | Who You Can See |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **FREE** | Other FREE members |
| **SELECT** | FREE and SELECT members |
| **SUMMIT** | FREE, SELECT, and SUMMIT members |

---

## Getting Started with Discovery

### Step 1: Complete Your Profile

A complete profile appears higher in search results:
- **FREE**: Basic info (name, location, photo)
- **SELECT+**: Add professional and golf identity

### Step 2: Set Your Preferences

Go to **Network → Discover Settings** to set:
- Preferred locations
- Handicap range
- Play frequency
- Professional industries of interest

### Step 3: Start Exploring

Navigate to **Network → Discover** to:
- Browse recommended members
- Search with filters
- View trending golfers
- See new members in your area

## Search Methods

### Basic Search (FREE)

Free members can search by:
- Location (city, state)
- Availability
- Basic profile info

**Limit**: 20 results per search

### Advanced Search (SELECT+)

SELECT+ members unlock:
- Professional filters (company, industry, experience)
- Golf filters (handicap range, home course, play frequency)
- Sort by reputation, handicap, or joined date
- Unlimited results

### Geographic Discovery

#### Nearby Search

Find members within a radius:
1. Go to **Network → Discover → Nearby**
2. Allow location access or enter a location
3. Adjust radius (5-100 miles)
4. Browse members sorted by distance

#### By City

Search specific cities:
1. Enter city name in search
2. Filter by state if needed
3. Results show all members in that city

### Course-Based Discovery

Find members who play at specific courses:
1. Go to **Golf → Courses**
2. Select a course
3. Tap **"Members Who Play Here"**
4. View and connect with fellow golfers

**Tip**: Golfers who play at the same course often have similar schedules.

## Smart Recommendations

### How Recommendations Work

Spotter's algorithm suggests members based on:
- **Profile similarity**: Similar handicap, location, experience
- **Mutual connections**: Friends of friends
- **Activity patterns**: When and where you play
- **Professional overlap**: Same industry or company type

### Improving Your Recommendations

1. **Complete your profile**: More data = better matches
2. **Play rounds**: Activity improves recommendations
3. **Build connections**: Network size affects suggestions
4. **Set preferences**: Tell us what you're looking for

### Introduction Requests (SELECT+)

Can't directly connect with someone interesting? Request an introduction:
1. Find a mutual connection
2. Tap **"Request Introduction"**
3. Explain why you'd like to connect
4. Your mutual connection can approve the intro

### Finding Members by Industry

1. Go to **Network → Discover → By Industry**
2. Select industries (Technology, Finance, Healthcare, etc.)
3. Browse professionals in your tier
4. Send connection requests with business context

### Company Connections

Spotter helps you find colleagues who golf:
1. Add your company in profile
2. Spotter suggests coworkers (if verified)
3. Build your workplace golf group

### Best Practices for Professional Networking

**Do:**
- Mention shared professional interests
- Suggest business + golf meetings
- Offer value (introductions, expertise)

**Don't:**
- Lead with sales pitches
- Connect solely for business
- Ignore the golf context

## Search Strategies

### Strategy 1: The Regular Group

**Goal**: Build a consistent playing group

**Approach**:
1. Search your home course
2. Filter by "weekly" play frequency
3. Connect with 10-15 potential regulars
4. Create a private round for testing compatibility
5. Keep the best matches

### Strategy 2: The Skill Builder

**Goal**: Improve your game with better players

**Approach**:
1. Search within your area
2. Filter for handicaps 5-10 points better than yours
3. Join their rounds (learn from watching)
4. Request connections post-round
5. Build mentor relationships

### Strategy 3: The Business Network

**Goal**: Expand professional network

**Approach**:
1. Search by your target industries
2. Look for decision-makers (titles)
3. Request connections with professional context
4. Suggest business + golf meetings
5. Follow up after rounds

### Strategy 4: The Social Golfer

**Goal**: Meet new people and have fun

**Approach**:
1. Browse trending members
2. Join public rounds
3. Connect with friendly, active golfers
4. Build diverse network
5. Always bring positive energy

## Understanding Match Scores

### What the Numbers Mean

Match scores (0-100) indicate compatibility:
- **90-100**: Excellent match (multiple alignments)
- **70-89**: Good match (some shared interests)
- **50-69**: Moderate match (some common ground)
- **Below 50**: Basic match (limited overlap)

### Factors Affecting Score

| Factor | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Handicap proximity | High | Similar skill levels |
| Location | High | Same city/area |
| Play frequency | Medium | Compatible schedules |
| Industry | Medium | Professional overlap |
| Mutual connections | Medium | Network effects |
| Profile completeness | Low | Shows engagement |

## Connection Request Best Practices

### Personalize Your Request

**Generic (Avoid):**
> "Hi, want to connect?"

**Better:**
> "Hi! I noticed we both play at TPC Scottsdale weekly. Would love to join one of your rounds sometime!"

**Best:**
> "Hey! Saw you're in tech and play at Silverleaf. I'm also in SaaS and looking for regular weekend games. Interested in connecting?"

### When to Connect

**Timing matters:**
- After playing together (mention the round)
- When joining someone's round
- Via introduction from mutual connection
- When profiles show clear compatibility

**Avoid:**
- Mass connection requests
- Requests without context
- Connecting only for business

## Tier-Specific Discovery Tips

### FREE Tier Tips

- **Be strategic**: 20 results per search, 50 connections max
- **Focus locally**: Prioritize nearby golfers
- **Complete profile**: Essential for being found
- **Join rounds**: Meet people organically
- **Quality over quantity**: Choose connections carefully

### SELECT Tier Tips

- **Use all filters**: Professional and golf identity
- **Unlimited search**: Explore broadly
- **Send intros**: Help others connect
- **Create rounds**: Attract similar players
- **Build network**: 500 connections to fill

### SUMMIT Tier Tips

- **Priority placement**: You appear first in results
- **Use boosts**: Get seen by more members
- **Set examples**: Your profile is a model
- **Mentor others**: Help newer members
- **Lead rounds**: Create exclusive experiences

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Discovery Don'ts

1. **Ignoring profile completeness**
   - Empty profiles get fewer views
   - Complete = trustworthy

2. **Mass connection requests**
   - Low acceptance rates
   - Looks spammy

3. **Generic messages**
   - Get ignored
   - Show you didn't read their profile

4. **Only connecting "up"**
   - Miss great golfers in your tier
   - Networking works both ways

5. **Forgetting to follow up**
   - Connections need nurturing
   - Schedule rounds within 2 weeks

## Troubleshooting Discovery

### "No Results Found"

**Possible causes:**
- Filters too restrictive
- Searching outside your tier
- New area with few members

**Solutions:**
- Broaden filters
- Expand search radius
- Invite friends to join Spotter

### "Not Getting Connection Requests"

**Possible causes:**
- Incomplete profile
- Low reputation score
- Not actively engaged

**Solutions:**
- Complete all profile sections
- Play more rounds
- Be proactive in sending requests

### "Too Many Options"

**Solution:**
- Refine filters
- Focus on specific goals
- Prioritize mutual connections

## FAQ

**Q: Can I search outside my tier?**
A: You can see higher tiers, but you can only connect with your tier.

**Q: Why can't I see some members?**
A: They may be in a lower tier, have blocked you, or have restricted visibility.

**Q: How do I appear higher in search results?**
A: Complete your profile, stay active, and build your reputation.

**Q: Can I hide from search?**
A: Yes, go to Profile → Settings → Privacy to adjust visibility.

**Q: What's the best time to send connection requests?**
A: Weekday evenings and weekend mornings have highest acceptance rates.
