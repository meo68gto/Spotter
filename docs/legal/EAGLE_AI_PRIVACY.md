## 1. Scope of This Addendum

This addendum applies to all users of Spotter's Eagle AI golf coaching feature ("Eagle AI"), which analyzes swing videos and pose keypoints to generate personalized coaching content (tips, drills, practice plans, and corrective cues).

If you do not use Eagle AI, this addendum does not apply to you.

---

## 2. What Data Eagle AI Collects

Eagle AI collects the following categories of personal data in connection with its coaching engine:

### 2.1 Swing Video Data
- Video recordings of your golf swing captured through Spotter's camera integration
- Video is processed locally on your device; frames are extracted for analysis
- Raw video files are stored in Supabase (see Section 5 for retention)

### 2.2 Pose Keypoints and Biomechanical Data
- Skeletal position data extracted from swing video frames (EP-01 — Pose Extraction)
- Joint angles, swing plane geometry, and timing data derived from pose analysis (EP-02 — Swing Phase Classifier)
- This constitutes **biometric-adjacent data** under GDPR, CCPA, and potentially BIPA (Illinois)

### 2.3 Fault and Coaching Analysis Data
- Detected swing faults and fault severity classifications (EP-03 — Fault Detection)
- AI-generated coaching tips, corrective cues, and drill recommendations
- Verification scores (V1–V5) generated by Eagle AI's self-verification loop
- Drill library citations (EP-16) referenced in coaching output

### 2.4 Coaching History
- Record of coaching sessions, including which drills were recommended and completed
- Practice plan history and drill completion outcomes
- User feedback on coaching recommendations (if provided)

### 2.5 Account and Profile Data
- User account identifier linking all above data to your Spotter account
- Golf skill level, stated goals, and preferences (if provided during onboarding)

## 3. How We Use Your Data

Your Eagle AI data is used solely for the following purposes:

| Purpose | Legal Basis (GDPR) | Data Used |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| Generate personalized coaching tips and drill recommendations | Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b)) + explicit consent (Art. 9(2)(a)) | Swing video, pose keypoints, fault data |

| Improve Eagle AI coaching engine accuracy and safety | Explicit consent (Art. 9(2)(a)) | Aggregated, anonymized coaching data |
| Comply with legal obligations | Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)) | As required by applicable law |

**We do not sell or share your Eagle AI data for advertising or marketing purposes.**

## 4. Consent Mechanism

### 4.1 Explicit Opt-In Required

Before Eagle AI analyzes any of your swing data, you must provide **explicit affirmative consent**. This consent:

- Is obtained via a dedicated consent screen in the Spotter app
- Clearly describes what data is collected, how it is analyzed, and how it is used
- Is separate from general app Terms of Service acceptance
- Can be withdrawn at any time (see Section 7.2)

### 4.2 Consent Screen Content

The Eagle AI consent screen will include:

> *"To provide personalized golf coaching, Eagle AI analyzes your swing video and extracts body position data (pose keypoints). This data is biometric-adjacent and is subject to the Eagle AI Privacy Addendum. You must be 13 years or older to use Eagle AI. AI-generated coaching is informational only and is not a substitute for professional golf instruction."*

### 4.3 Children Under 13

Eagle AI is **not available to users under 13 years of age**. Users who identify as under 13 during onboarding will not be permitted to use the Eagle AI feature. This age threshold is consistent with COPPA (13+ for US) and the general threshold under GDPR (16+, with parental consent available at 13+ in most member states).

## 6. Third-Party Data Sharing

### 6.1 Supabase (Database)

Swing videos, pose keypoints, and coaching data are stored in Supabase (Spotter's primary database). Supabase acts as a data processor under GDPR. Their data processing agreement (DPA) must cover biometric-adjacent data.

**[ACTION REQUIRED — LEGAL REVIEW]:** Verify Supabase DPA covers biometric data categories under GDPR Article 9 and CCPA/CPRA personal information definitions.

### 6.2 Ollama (Local Inference)

Swing video analysis and coaching generation run via **Ollama on local Apple Silicon hardware** (Spotter backend server). Video frames and pose data are processed locally; no swing video data is sent to any external inference API.

### 6.3 No Other Third-Party Sharing

Eagle AI coaching data is **not shared with any other third parties**, including:
- No advertising networks
- No data brokers
- No external AI or analytics services beyond Supabase (as data processor)
- No public MCP server access — Eagle AI is internal to Spotter only

## 8. BIPA — Illinois Users

This section applies specifically to users located in Illinois.

### 8.1 Biometric Data Defined Under BIPA

Under the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA), "biometric identifiers" include retina or iris scans, fingerprints, voiceprints, and **scans of hand or face geometry**. Pose keypoints extracted from golf swing video — specifically body geometry and joint positioning data — **likely constitute biometric identifiers** under BIPA.

### 8.2 BIPA Requirements

In compliance with BIPA, Spotter will:

1. **Inform users in writing** before collecting biometric identifiers — this Privacy Addendum serves that purpose
2. **Obtain written release** from users before collecting biometric identifiers — the Eagle AI consent screen (Section 4) serves that purpose
3. **Not profit** from biometric data — Eagle AI data is not sold or shared
4. **Store biometric data using reasonable care** — encryption at rest in Supabase; no external transmission
5. **Not disclose biometric data** except as required by law or with explicit user consent

### 8.3 BIPA Damages

BIPA provides statutory damages of **$1,000 per negligent violation** and **$5,000 per intentional or reckless violation**. These apply to unauthorized collection, storage, or disclosure of biometric identifiers without proper notice and consent.

**[CRITICAL]:** Failure to implement the consent flow in Section 4 before enabling Eagle AI analysis for Illinois users creates direct BIPA liability exposure. Do not launch Eagle AI for Illinois users without the consent mechanism fully operational.

## 9. GDPR — EU/EEA Users

### 9.1 Legal Basis for Processing

Biometric-adjacent data (pose keypoints) qualifies as **special category data** under GDPR Article 9. Processing requires one of:
- **Explicit consent** (Art. 9(2)(a)) — primary basis for Eagle AI
- **Legitimate use directly related to coaching services** (Art. 9(2)(h)) — for core coaching delivery

### 9.2 Data Subject Rights Under GDPR

EU/EEA users have all rights listed in Section 7, plus:
- **Right to lodge a complaint** with a supervisory authority (Art. 77) — e.g., local DPA
- **Right to restriction of processing** (Art. 18) where applicable
- **Right to object** to processing based on legitimate interests (Art. 21)

### 9.3 Data Residency Note

Supabase is a cloud-hosted service. For EU users, this means swing video and coaching data is transferred outside the EEA. Spotter relies on:
- Supabase Standard/DCA agreements with GDPR-compliant data processing terms
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) if any data transfers occur outside covered DPAs

## 10. CCPA/CPRA — California Users

### 10.1 Categories of Personal Information

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), Eagle AI collects the following categories of personal information:

- **Biometric information** (pose keypoints, body geometry from swing video) — sensitive personal information under CPRA
- **Audio/visual information** (swing video recordings)
- **Activity data** (drill completion, practice history, coaching interactions)
- **Identified inferences** (coaching profile, skill level, swing fault analysis)

### 10.2 CPRA Sensitive Personal Information

Pose keypoints and fault analysis constitute **sensitive personal information** under CPRA Section 1798.121. This requires:
- Clear disclosure of the purpose of collection
- Limiting use to the disclosed purpose
- No opt-out mechanism required for SPI (already regulated separately)

### 10.3 Your CPRA Rights

California residents have the right to:
- Know what personal information is collected and how it is used
- Request deletion of personal information
- Request correction of inaccurate personal information
- Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (N/A — we do not sell/share)
- Limit use of sensitive personal information (applies to biometric data)

To submit CPRA requests: **[REVIEW: privacy@spotter.golf or in-app privacy center — per main Privacy Policy]**

### 10.4 Financial Incentives

Eagle AI coaching is provided as a feature of Spotter's service. To the extent any portion of Spotter's service involving Eagle AI data is considered a "financial incentive" program under CPRA, the relevant notice will be provided.

## 11. Security

Spotter implements the following security measures for Eagle AI data:

- **Encryption at rest:** All swing video and pose data encrypted in Supabase
- **Encryption in transit:** All data transmitted between Spotter app and backend via TLS
- **Access controls:** Eagle AI coaching data accessible only to authenticated Spotter backend services; not exposed via public API
- **MCP server internal only:** Eagle AI MCP server is not exposed externally; it is an internal Spotter component only
- **Audit logging:** All data access and deletion requests logged
